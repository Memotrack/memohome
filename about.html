About MemoTrack

Most hospital software are built by developers who understand technology but not hospitals. MemoTrack derived as a strategy from the ground level — shaped by the one who works in the hospital, saw the problems in practice and applied with technical skill to build a product that achieved measurable success in real time.

Who We Are

Centre for Research and Innovation in Health Tech (CRIHT)

A unit of Yauvana Yaazh Aayvagam Private Limited

We are a healthcare technology company based in Tamil Nadu, building applied software for hospitals, medical colleges, and clinical practitioners. Our work spans hospital operations, clinical education, patient records, nutrition management, and AI-enabled healthcare tools.

We do not build generic software and adapt it for hospitals. We build for hospitals first, and nothing else. Our products are designed for Indian conditions — and built to global quality standards, with instant scalability for international deployment.

The Founder

Dr. R K Ramanan, MBBS., MMST (IIT Kharagpur)

MBBS, Master's in Medical Science and Technology from IIT Kharagpur — the first physician-scientist training program of its kind in India. He assesses clinical workflows as a practising doctor, architects technical solutions as an IIT-trained engineer, and understands hospital realities from working inside them. Every product CRIHT builds carries that combined perspective.

Our Principles

🔓No feature paywalls

Every hospital gets every feature on every plan. Voice input, verification systems, real-time dashboards, and administrative tools are not premium additions.

🌍Multi-language, globally deployable

Multi-language voice input. SMS OTP authentication that works without smartphones. Offline-capable where needed. Built for variable connectivity and shift-based workforces.

✓Global quality standard

Every product is built to international benchmarks — not a localised workaround, but a product that can stand beside global-market alternatives.

⏱Built to run continuously

Hospitals operate 24 hours a day. Downtime is not an inconvenience — in a clinical environment, it is a safety event. Every product is built and maintained with that standard.
